FINDINGS

-Damaged Shingles
Composition shingles protect the roof from water and debris, preventing damage and leaks. With age shingles can have granular degradation that will then expose the fiberglass and asphalt material of shingle. This is a good indication of a roofing system useful life. Other damage to shingles can be caused by wind and tree damage.

-Valley Flashings
Valley Flashings protect underlayment at roof valleys, allowing water to flow over the underlayment per installation code. Excessive debris can obstruct the flow and push water off the flashing and on to the underlayment.

-Dry Rot
Dry Rot is fungal wood decay most often caused by water intrusion and lack of air flow in between roof tiles and underlayment. Dry rot can lead to significant roof damage and requires the area to be opened up, removed and replaced.
