Article 6. Scope of Work:

  1. Subcontractor agrees to provide the following services for each project:
    1. Presite and measure the project and provide a formal proposal for work per home improvement contract provided by Contractor.
    2. Submit a complete list of equipment and material to be provided by Contractor at the time a proposal is submitted.
    3. Submit for permitting and obtain permit from AHJ. Copies of permits and fee receipts shall be sent via email to Contractor.
    4. Coordinate and schedule installation of work with Contractor's installation coordinator.
    5. Provide labor services to complete installation of roofing products.
    6. Schedule and complete inspections with AHJ and obtain final sign-off of permit.
    7. Provide Contractor with shop drawings showing any field revisions or changes not included in original contract. This shall be submitted with final invoicing for the project.
    8. Warranty all workmanship for the period of 10 years from the date of installation.
    9. Take pictures of project before commencement of work and upon completion of work and submit pictures to Contractor with invoicing for the project. Invoices will not be accepted without attached pictures.
  2. Contractor agrees to provide the following:
    1. Provide to Subcontractor a scope of work and specifications required to provide a proposal and material list to complete work. This is typically a copy of the home improvement contract.
    2. Coordinate with Subcontractor and customer to ensure timely and efficient commencement and completion of the project.
    3. Reimburse Subcontractor for permit fees upon receipt of invoice with fee receipt from AHJ attached.
  3. Job Performance Standards
    1. Specifications to be per home improvement contract between Contractor and property owner.
    2. Remove and haul away existing roofing, underlayment, gutters and downspouts as per home improvement contract.
    3. Inspect existing roof trusses, framing, decking and fascia for dry-rot. Dry-rot repair to include 4 man hours and $250 in material at no additional cost. Costs for additional dry-rot repair shall be submitted and approved by Contractor prior to commencement of the repairs.
    4. Install ice & water shield under valley metal and on eaves. Ice & water shield shall extend a minimum of six inches past the edge of valley metal and past the transition from attic to eave.
    5. Install valley metal in all valleys. Subcontractor to paint valley metal to match new roofing material.
    6. Install all new wall to roof flashing, step flashing, penetration flashings and edge metal. Subcontractor to paint flashings and edge metal to match new roofing material.
    7. Install 2x2 nosing on all gables and gutters.
    8. Install ridge ventilation or dormer vents to meet local AHJ requirements and codes.
    9. Install synthetic underlayment per manufacturer's recommendations.
    10. Install starter shingles on gutters and gables.
    11. Install finish roofing material per manufacturer's recommendations.
    12. Clean up and haul away all trash and debris.
    13. Use a magnet to clean up all nails, screws, staples, and metal debris.
    14. Clean out all dirt and construction debris at the completion of work.
    15. Obtain final permit sign-off from AHJ.
    16. Provide Contractor with job completion package including warranty information, installation certificate, and completed permit paperwork.
