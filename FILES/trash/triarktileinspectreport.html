FINDINGS


-Damaged Tile
Roof Tiles are the main defense against rain and debris on the roof and can be cracked by wind damage or foot traffic. Depending on damage, some broken tiles need replacement while some only need to be repaired with caulking. Another concern is slipped tiles, which are not damaged, but need to be re-positioned to prevent water and sun exposure to roof underlayment.

-Rubber Collars
The plumbing vent pipes on the top of the home are fitted with rubber collars to prevent leaks, but these collars are often damaged or missing entirely and need to be replaced to prevent water from entering the sides of roof jack flashing.

-Sidewall, Chimney, and Roof Jacks
Roof flashings protect underlayment where the roof meets the chimney, or any roof jacks or sidewalls. Improper installation or debris buildup negates this protection and leads to water damage. Repairs in these areas require the roof to be opened up to remove debris and re-secure flashings.

-Valley Flashings
Valley Flashings protect underlayment at roof valleys, allowing water to flow over the underlayment per installation code. Excessive debris can obstruct the flow and push water off the flashing and on to the underlayment. Repairs in these areas requires the valley to be opened up for debris to be removed.

-Dry Rot
Dry Rot is fungal wood decay most often caused by water intrusion and lack of air flow in between roof tiles and underlayment. Dry rot can lead to significant roof damage and requires the area to be opened up, removed and replaced.
